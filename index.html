Philadelphia Retail Suitability Engine

Research & Methodology

Drawing on my experience in the Sharma Group, where I developed the ability to rapidly break into and contribute to new technical fields, I independently researched and implemented the 11-stage data science pipeline taxonomy defined by Biswas et al. (ICSE '22). This high-impact paper (125+ citations) serves as the architectural backbone of this project, ensuring a strictly decoupled and reproducible workflow.

The Suitability Model

Designed for high-density commercial corridors like Center City, the engine solves for site suitability by balancing accessibility against market saturation. Using a normalized suitability equation, the model treats population as a uniform constant to isolate the relationship between transit flow and competitive pressure:

$$S = 0.7T - 0.3C$$

  • Transit Score ($T$): Quantifies "Kinetic Flow" using rail-weighted spatial joins from SEPTA’s 2025 infrastructure data.
  • Competition Intensity ($C$): Quantifies "Potential Resistance" by calculating the product of competitor ratings and review volume via the Google Places API.

The 11-Stage Pipeline Architecture

Implementing the Biswas Taxonomy: [ACQ] Ingestion → [PRP] Standardizing (EPSG:2272) → [STR] SQLite Persistence → [FTA] Feature Engineering → [TRS] Min-Max Scaling → [SLT] Quality Filtering → [MDL] Objective Solving → [EVL] Statistical Validation → [INT] Market Grading → [CMN] Data Publishing → [DPL] Web Portal Deployment.
